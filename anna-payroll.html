Payroll for ANNA Money app

I led experience design for a tool that generated a new stream of active users for ANNA Money.

The challenge

The goal of this project was to rapidly release a solution for business owners to have their payroll filed accurately, cheaply, and without pain.

Research initiatives

I used existing customer research and I gathered new insight to understand our the audience and the problem more deeply.

Persona, to walk a mile in the user’s shoes

I created a new target persona that was relevant to a person who would need to file payroll. I generated this from interviews and research I had previously conducted and synthesised along with other design team members.

We now had a vision of who the user was, what frustrates them, and what opportunities lie to help with their problems.

Bio – Business owner Frustrations – Being time poor; doing paperwork; time are tough; want to save funds. it's expensive to hire an accountant, and don't want to overspend on taxes. Motivations – Likes this and that

Additional research to establish customer needs

To zoom into payroll and understand more relevant pain points, I contacted customers in our Research Customer Panel and asked them a few questions regarding their perspective on payroll. Some specific problems came out of the conversations. Here are some of the key takeaways:

Show me how to run payroll – I lack the acumen on how to get set up and run it correctly.Make sure I don’t miss a deadline - I’m afraid of forgetting to pay the employee and HMRC on time (which could result in a fine).Prevent me from getting in trouble - Make sure I pay the employee a reasonable amount and send the correct PAYE to HMRC, and not get a fine.

The problem and ideation

The problem we focussed on: Business owners want to file payroll themselves, so they can save money on paying an accountant. But they don't know how to do it, and they're worried about doing it wrong and getting into trouble with the tax office.

Comp Analysis. Research into current solutions and what they could currently offer customers.Be user-centric, and create powerfully unique solutions but don’t always need to reinvent the wheel, also great to see design patterns. What did I learn?Xyz summary
