Bookkeeping Score for the ANNA Money app

I designed a tool that makes boring tasks fun, and I boosted subscriptions for the company.

My role was to:

Year

• Conduct experience design
• Achieve product goals
• Guide a visual designer

2023

Admin made fun

Our product team set out to create a tool that customers would use to keep their books tidy – and be happy to pay for it. I thought creatively and designed solutions that captured engagement in what’s normally a dull area.

Chat interactions

ANNA uses conversation design for many of it’s features. I designed flows, wrote copy, and worked with a visual designer to create important touchpoints for the Bookkeeping Score, including feature awareness and weekly reminders.

Wireframes Flows Copywriting

Tinder-style admin

I leveraged the ‘swipe right’ UX pattern found in many digital tools, making it simple and pleasant to complete tasks. This became a great way to boil down complex information and provide focus for the user. The pattern reduces cognitive load.

Wireframes Flows

Reward mechanics

I made sure users feel rewarded for their bookkeeping work. They reach a high score, feel at peace, and they save money on taxes.

Process examples

Early user testing

I created a clickable prototype using Figma, recruited testers in our customer base and booked them in for interviews. I recorded our testing sessions via Zoom and summarised their responses. I shared the results with the design team before making improvements.

Detailed wireframes

I made detailed wireframes. With an eye on the design system and current live components, checking what we could re-use and what new components needed to be created. As there was a dedicated visual designer available for this particular project, I supplied specific information for new components.

Working with Data Science

I shared the vision with Data Science and we collaborated to fine-tune a system that would generate a great experience. They created a proof of concept in a spreadsheet and together we pinpointed the ideal task volume prior to release.

Mapping results to the flow

I created user flows to show what key pages would be required for development. Later in the project I added performance results against pages so that we could see the strengths and weaknesses.

How did we do?

The feature performs well. Outreach conversion is high, engagement of the tool is healthy, and there are a valuable number of users upgrading to a paid plan to keep the feature after their trial period.
